# Earned Wage Access (EWA) Policy

## Eligibility

To be eligible for an Earned Wage Access advance, an employee must meet the following criteria:

- Must have completed the **3-month probation period**.
- Must be an **active, permanent employee** (not terminated or on notice).
- Must have **approved timesheet hours** in the current pay period.
- Must not have any **unresolved disciplinary actions**.

## Limits

- Employees may access up to **50%** of their earned wages for the current pay period.
- The maximum advance per pay period is **R5,000**, regardless of earned amount.
- The available amount is reduced by any **outstanding (undisbursed) EWA balance** from the current period.

## Fees

- A flat fee of **R10** is charged per EWA transaction.
- The fee is deducted from the advance amount (e.g., a R1,500 advance results in R1,490 received).
- There are no additional interest charges or hidden fees.

## Repayment

- EWA advances are automatically deducted from the employee's next payroll.
- The full advance amount (not including the fee) is deducted from gross pay.
- If the employee's net pay after deduction is insufficient, the remaining balance carries over to the following pay period.

## Restrictions

- EWA advances are not available during the **first 3 months** of employment.
- Employees on **suspension** or under **disciplinary proceedings** are not eligible.
- EWA is available only for the **current pay period** - future earnings cannot be accessed.
- A maximum of **3 EWA transactions** per pay period is allowed.
